# Canonical Domain Charter v1.0  
LightBorn Nexus Ecosystem

---

## Overview

This charter defines the canonical domain structure of the LightBorn Nexus ecosystem.  
Each domain is distinct, non-overlapping, and governed by clear function, output, and transition rules.

The system preserves structural integrity by separating:
- creation
- governance
- teaching
- publication
- public expression

---

## Domains

---

### LN — LightBorn Nexus  
**Function:** Generative Source Field  

**Purpose:**  
Private field for inquiry, synthesis, and early-stage creation prior to institutionalization.

**What belongs here:**  
- Raw ideas, notes, drafts  
- Experiments, reflections, early frameworks  
- Unresolved or evolving thinking  

**What does NOT belong:**  
- Finalized teachings or curriculum  
- Public-facing or published materials  
- Institutional policy or canon  

**Output type:**  
Exploratory, non-final, non-public material  

**Transition rule:**  
Moves forward only through explicit founder selection and readiness judgment → enters SWI  

---

### SWI — SoulWork Institute  
**Function:** Governance + Canon Formation  

**Purpose:**  
Institutional authority responsible for approving, refining, and stabilizing knowledge into canon.

**What belongs here:**  
- Approved frameworks and teachings  
- Curriculum structures  
- Institutional language, standards, and policies  

**What does NOT belong:**  
- Raw drafts or exploratory thinking  
- Informal or unreviewed material  
- Delivery-specific formatting (courses, publications, media)  

**Output type:**  
Governed, teachable, institutionally aligned content  

**Transition rule:**  
Once approved, content is routed to a downstream domain based on function (SWL or SWP)  

---

### SWL — SoulWork Learning  
**Function:** Pedagogical Delivery  

**Purpose:**  
Structured learning and educational delivery.

**What belongs here:**  
- Courses, programs, pathways  
- Circles, labs, fellowships  
- Sequenced educational experiences  

**What does NOT belong:**  
- Governance or policy decisions  
- Publication-ready manuscripts  
- Unstructured or unapproved material  

**Output type:**  
Learning experiences and curriculum implementations  

**Transition rule:**  
Receives only SWI-approved material → structures into pedagogical form → delivers to participants  

---

### SWP — SoulWork Publications  
**Function:** Codification + Intellectual Property  

**Purpose:**  
Preservation, authorship, and publication of codified knowledge.

**What belongs here:**  
- Books, manuscripts, and long-form written works  
- Formal written frameworks prepared for publication  
- Codified teachings intended as enduring intellectual property  
- Structured written knowledge artifacts  

**What does NOT belong:**  
- Speaking, workshops, or live teaching  
- Media appearances or informal public communication  
- Draft-stage or unapproved material  

**Output type:**  
Published or publication-ready written works (intellectual property)  

**Transition rule:**  
Receives SWI-approved material → undergoes authorship, refinement, and codification → moves to publication and archival form  

---

### SWPL — SoulWork Public Leadership  
**Function:** Public Expression + Amplification  

**Purpose:**  
Outward-facing communication, visibility, and thought leadership.

**What belongs here:**  
- Speaking, keynotes, workshops  
- Media, interviews, podcasts  
- Public communication and thought leadership  

**What does NOT belong:**  
- Canon formation or governance  
- Curriculum structures  
- Core intellectual property artifacts (which belong in SWP)  

**Output type:**  
Public-facing expression and communication  

**Transition rule:**  
Draws from SWI-approved material → adapts for context → delivers outward to audiences  

---

### SWIL — Reserved Domain  
**Function:** Future Capacity (Inactive)  

**Purpose:**  
Reserved structural space for future expansion.

**What belongs here:**  
- Nothing until formally defined  

**What does NOT belong:**  
- Any active content, workflow, or dependency  

**Output type:**  
None  

**Transition rule:**  
No transitions permitted until domain is explicitly defined and integrated  

---

## System Flow

LN → SWI → (SWL or SWP)
         ↘
          SWPL (parallel outward expression)

---

## Structural Principles

- LN = creation  
- SWI = authority  
- SWL = learning delivery  
- SWP = publication and intellectual property  
- SWPL = public expression and amplification  
- SWIL = reserved capacity  

All domains remain:
- distinct  
- sequential where required  
- non-overlapping
